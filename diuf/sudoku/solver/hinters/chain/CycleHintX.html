Bidirectional X-Cycle{2}

The cells {0} form a bidirectional cycle: there are exactly two ways of placing the value {1} in these cells, forming two different possible configurations. Some {1}s appear in a box, row, or col regardless of which configuration is correct, and one of these two configurations must be correct, hence other occurrences of the value {1} can be removed from these regions.

Therefore we can remove {5}.

This is a Bidirectional X-Cycle, involving regions with two places for a value. X-Cycles are also known as Fishy Cycles or Bilocation cycles.
